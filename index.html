Guide

New to research? Start here.

  1. 1 Pick a topic you’re curious about — use the sidebar to filter by field. Don’t overthink it.
  2. 2 Toggle “Beginner-friendly” to narrow to labs known to welcome students with no prior research experience.
  3. 3 Open 3–5 lab cards that look interesting. Click “Lab website” and skim one recent paper or the lab’s research page.
  4. 4 Use “Draft email” to generate a starting template, then rewrite it entirely in your own voice. Mention a specific paper or finding.
  5. 5 Send 3–5 emails per week. Follow up once after 10 days. Most responses come within two weeks.

Tip: the single best thing you can do is mention a specific experiment or finding from the lab. PIs notice this immediately.

About

Yale Research Match is a student-built tool that indexes labs from YURA, the YSM Lab Index, and FAS/SEAS/YSPH department directories. Data is curated for undergrad usability: recruiting status, working style, beginner-friendliness, and direct contact info where available.

Lab info may change — always verify recruiting status directly with the PI or lab manager before reaching out.
