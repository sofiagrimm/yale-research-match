🌱 New to research? Here's how to use this in 15 minutes.

Most undergrads don't know how to approach lab search — and many PIs have never heard from you before. That's okay. Here's a simple process:

  1. Pick a broad area you care about — e.g. cancer biology, climate, AI in medicine, neuroscience, aging. Don't over-optimize yet.
  2. Filter to beginner-friendly + actively recruiting using the checkboxes above. This narrows 310 labs to a realistic shortlist.
  3. Open 3–5 lab cards that look interesting. Skim the description and tags. Click "Lab website" to read a recent paper or their "Research" page.
  4. Use "Draft email to this PI" on a lab that seems like a fit. Fill in your year, major, and what interests you — then rewrite it in your own voice. Do not copy-paste.
  5. Send a small, thoughtful batch of 3–5 emails per week rather than one mass send. Follow up once after 10 days if no reply.

💡 The single best thing you can do: read one paper from the lab and mention a specific experiment or finding that interested you. PIs notice this immediately.
